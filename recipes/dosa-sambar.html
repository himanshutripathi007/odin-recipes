Masala Dosa

Description

Masala Dosa is a crispy and golden South Indian pancake made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, stuffed with a spiced potato filling. It is traditionally served with tangy sambar and coconut chutney.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dosa batter (available in stores or homemade)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • 4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon oil for tempering

Steps

  1. In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
  2. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and green chilies. Saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric powder, mashed potatoes, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Finish by adding chopped coriander leaves and remove from heat.
  5. Now, make the dosa on a hot griddle (tawa) using the dosa batter. Spread it thin and cook until crispy.
  6. Once the dosa is golden brown, add a spoonful of the potato masala to the center.
  7. Fold the dosa into a roll or a half-moon shape and serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
